Review: Brain Works (두뇌공조) [K-drama 2023]

By ASRreviews

Song of choice: Hooverphonic — Mad About You

📺 Brain Works (두뇌공조) — a quirky science-meets-crime ensemble from KBS2 starring Jung Yong-hwa and Cha Tae-hyun — blends comedy, mystery, and neuro-themed storytelling into a drama that’s equal parts fun and… divisive.

First Impressions

Brain Works sets up an oddball duo:
Shin Ha-ru (Jung Yong-hwa) — brilliant yet arrogant neuroscientist obsessed with psychopathy studies
Geum Myung-se (Cha Tae-hyun) — kindhearted detective with a deeply altruistic worldview

Their chemistry of course forms the backbone of the series — mismatched brains literally and figuratively teamed to solve bizarre cases involving neurological conditions. The supporting characters shine in their own right, with my personal favorites being the quietly compelling duo: Woo Hyun and Kwak Sun Youn.

Good and Bad

Pros
 Refreshing genre mix — few dramas engage brain science with comedy and crime investigation so boldly. 
 Performances, especially the bromantic energy between the leads, earn most viewers’ affection.

Cons
Many critics felt the writing wavers — with filler sequences and awkward moves between humor and mystery. 
 Some found the science premise stretched too thin, making it feel pseudointellectual at times.

Verdict from DramaRambles:

“Memorable at times, but also unmemorable… comedy, character work, and case balance could have been stronger.” — DramaRambles (rated ~2.8)

Audience Reactions // Reddit & Beyond

Reddit snippets give us the emotional kaleidoscope of fandom:

“Yes watch it it’s really good… science aspect and action are fun.”
 One Redditor genuinely enjoyed the scientific twist and the character growth of Dr. Shin.

“Simple, silly and entertaining… gets better.”
 Discussion threads from r/KDRAMA agree the drama is lightweight fun — not masterpiece territory, but enjoyable.

“Dropped after first episode.”
 Other commenters confess it didn’t grip them early on, or the case plots felt thin compared to stronger genre shows.

Highlights People Talk About

Bromance over romance — viewers often cite the friendship tension between the leads as the reason to watch.

Laughs and charm — comedic beats and oddball character flairs pick up the pace, even when the plot isn’t razor-sharp.

Intriguing case concepts — each episode’s neurological twist keeps curiosity alive, even if the execution falters.

Some Criticisms That Keep Coming Up

Uneven tone — between serious police procedurals and slapstick science, Brain Works sometimes feels conceptually disjointed.

Shallow case depth — fans of moodier or more detailed mysteries may find the episodic cases too surface-level.

Character arcs stronger than plot arcs — most memorable moments come from character interactions rather than the overarching mystery.

Final Take

Brain Works is the brainy oddball of K-drama season — a show that doesn’t take itself too seriously, loves its own zany neuroscience beats, and delivers some truly quirky interpersonal fireworks. It’s not perfect by critics’ standards, but if you’re into character-driven crime with a heavy dose of humor and heart, it’s worth the watch. And yes, plenty of bromance.

Rating: 7.9/10
