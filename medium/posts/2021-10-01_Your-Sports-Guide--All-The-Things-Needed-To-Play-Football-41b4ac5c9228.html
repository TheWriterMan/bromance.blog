Your Sports Guide: All The Things Needed To Play Football

Football is undoubtedly a great sport and its popularity is definitely no joke. There are thousands of people from around the world that fill the stadiums to witness the world cup and millions of other people who enjoy watching the live streaming with their loved ones in the comfort of their homes.

It’s not just the world cup but major small-scale football games keep on happening all year round. Schools and colleges are also not behind, they conduct regular football games on their own as well.

As a result, each year there are thousands of young people who aspire to become the next great footballer. (And you could be one of them.)

For someone who is just starting playing football, there are some important things you need, of course apart from a strong will and passion. These things are super-ultra important gears that are going to protect your body against any unenforceable injuries.

Unfortunately, most of these injuries are related to the brain and could cause irreversible life-impacting damage.

According to numerous studies, it was found out that former players have a high probability to suffer from a high prevalence of concussions, traumatic brain injuries called chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE), a severe brain condition.

Furthermore, sports-related ASDH (acute subdural hematoma) has been found out to be a leading cause of death and severe morbidity in popular contact sports like American football and Japanese judo.

That’s why when we look at the footballers wearing all that bulky equipment, it’s primarily to protect the players from getting severely hurt.

Here are some of the most basic and crucial equipment that you should invest in if you’re just starting out football:

Helmet

As most injuries are brain-related, isn’t it better to take extra precautions in order to safeguard yourselves from any avoidable injuries? There are a number of good brands out there but in my opinion, you can try the Riddle Helmet or the Schutt Air XP Pro. There are also some great options out there in the market that you can try but make sure to purchase one.

Neck Collar/Neck Roll

These protect you from any sudden whiplash moves that could cause severe neck or spine injury and thus, I would recommend buying one.

Mouth Guard

Although getting your tooth fixed is not that big of a problem but why go through the hassle when you can avoid it simply by using a mouthguard. There are a number of options available online that you can try. I would recommend the Shock Doctor Gel Max Mouth Guard.

Thigh, Hip, and Knee Pads

There are a number of severe injuries to your thigh, hip, or knee that can be avoided if only you wear these pads. Adequate pads (for hips, thigh, and knee) can protect you from injuries as they absorb the majority of the impact of the fall. While some are built into girdles, many can be put on via pockets or snap attachments.

Shoulder Pads

Shoulder pads are an important element of football safety equipment for players of all ages and ability levels. Even flag football players should wear high-quality shoulder pads for protection. They protect your shoulders, upper chest, and back during collisions, which can occur even if you’re not playing contact football.

Just Remember

Although a majority of players try to avoid these bulky pieces of equipment but remember that these pieces of equipment are for your own protection and well-being. None of it will matter if you can’t play because of a simple mistake. So, make sure to protect your future and avoid getting injured by using the abovementioned equipments.
