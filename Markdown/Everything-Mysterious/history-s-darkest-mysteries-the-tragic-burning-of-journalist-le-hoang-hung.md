Welcome to the fifth installment of our “History’s Darkest Mysteries” series. In this edition, we delve into a chilling case that shook Vietnam’s journalistic community and raised serious questions about press freedom and corruption: the brutal murder of Le Hoang Hung, a brave investigative reporter who paid the ultimate price for his dedication to uncovering the truth.

![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dtperak4e/image/upload/bromance-blog/medium-8930738bcb4c)**The Victim: Le Hoang Hung**Le Hoang Hung was a 50-year-old journalist working for Nguoi Lao Dong (The Laborer), a major newspaper based in Ho Chi Minh City. Known for his tenacious reporting on corruption and smuggling in the Mekong Delta region, Hung had built a reputation as a fearless investigator who wasn’t afraid to tackle sensitive issues.**The Crime: A Horrific Attack**On January 19, 2011, in the early hours of the morning, Le Hoang Hung was sleeping in his home in Tan An, the capital city of Long An province, about 45 kilometers from Ho Chi Minh City. At approximately 1:00 AM, an unknown assailant entered his bedroom and doused him with a flammable liquid, believed to be gasoline, before setting him on fire.

Hung’s wife, Tran Thi Thuy Lieu, who was sleeping in another room with their two children, was awakened by his screams. She rushed to find her husband engulfed in flames. Despite her efforts to extinguish the fire, Hung suffered severe burns over 50% of his body.**The Aftermath: A Nation in Shock**Le Hoang Hung was immediately rushed to Cho Ray Hospital in Ho Chi Minh City, where doctors fought to save his life. For ten agonizing days, Hung clung to life in the hospital’s intensive care unit. Despite the best efforts of medical staff, he succumbed to his injuries on January 30, 2011, leaving behind a grieving family and a shocked nation.

The brutal nature of the attack sent shockwaves through Vietnam’s media community and civil society. Journalists across the country expressed outrage and fear, recognizing that one of their own had likely been targeted for his work.**The Investigation: A Complex Web**The investigation into Hung’s murder was led by the Long An Provincial Police Department, with oversight from the Ministry of Public Security. From the outset, the case presented numerous challenges:

1. Lack of Witnesses: The attack occurred in the middle of the night, and no one outside the family had seen the assailant.

2. Limited Physical Evidence: The fire had destroyed much of the potential forensic evidence at the scene.

3. Multiple Potential Motives: Given Hung’s work as an investigative journalist, there were numerous individuals and groups who might have wanted to silence him.

The police pursued several lines of inquiry:

1. Professional Retaliation: Investigators looked into Hung’s recent reporting, particularly his work on corruption and smuggling cases in the Mekong Delta.

2. Personal Disputes: They also explored the possibility that the attack was related to personal conflicts rather than his journalistic work.

3. Random Violence: Though considered unlikely, the option of a random act of violence was not initially ruled out.

As the investigation progressed, attention increasingly focused on Hung’s personal life, leading to a surprising turn of events.**The Unexpected Twist: A Family Tragedy**In a development that shocked many following the case, on February 22, 2011, less than a month after Hung’s death, police arrested his wife, Tran Thi Thuy Lieu. Investigators alleged that Lieu had hired a man to kill her husband due to personal disputes and gambling debts.

According to the police report, Lieu had accumulated significant debts from online gambling, amounting to over 1 billion Vietnamese dong (approximately $47,000 at the time). Authorities claimed she had hired a man named Dang Van Nhat to kill her husband, allegedly paying him 80 million dong (about $3,800).**The Trial and Verdict**![](https://res.cloudinary.com/dtperak4e/image/upload/bromance-blog/medium-eb3a4d7f22d6)

The trial of Tran Thi Thuy Lieu began on November 28, 2011, at the People’s Court of Long An Province. During the proceedings, Lieu maintained her innocence, claiming she had been coerced into confessing. However, the alleged hitman, Dang Van Nhat, testified against her, providing details of the plot.

On December 1, 2011, after a four-day trial, Tran Thi Thuy Lieu was found guilty of murder and sentenced to 20 years in prison. Dang Van Nhat received 16 years for his role in the crime.**Controversy and Doubts**Despite the conviction, the case continued to raise questions and doubts among journalists and human rights activists:

1. Motive Questions: Many found it hard to believe that gambling debts were the sole motive for such a brutal crime.

2. Investigation Speed: Some observers noted the unusually quick resolution of the case, particularly given its initial complexity.

3. Coercion Claims: Lieu’s allegations of being coerced into a confession were never fully addressed in court.

4. Journalistic Angle Overlooked: Critics argued that the investigation too quickly dismissed the possibility that Hung was killed due to his reporting.**Legacy and Impact**The murder of Le Hoang Hung and its aftermath had a profound impact on Vietnam’s journalistic community:

1. Chilling Effect: Many reporters expressed increased fear about pursuing sensitive stories, particularly those involving corruption.

2. Press Freedom Debates: The case sparked renewed discussions about the dangers faced by investigative journalists in Vietnam.

3. Legal Reforms: Some advocates called for stronger protections for journalists and whistleblowers in the wake of Hung’s death.

4. Memorials: Hung’s colleagues at Nguoi Lao Dong and other media outlets held memorials, vowing to continue his legacy of fearless reporting.**Unanswered Questions**Despite the official resolution of the case, several questions continue to linger:

- Was the motive for Hung’s murder truly personal, or was there a connection to his investigative work?
- Were there other individuals involved in the crime who were never brought to justice?
- Did the rapid focus on Hung’s wife lead investigators to overlook other potential leads?
- How can Vietnam better protect its journalists from violence and intimidation?**Conclusion: A Mystery Partially Solved?**The case of Le Hoang Hung stands as a dark chapter in Vietnam’s recent history. While the official investigation concluded with convictions, the lingering doubts and unanswered questions continue to haunt many. Hung’s tragic death serves as a stark reminder of the risks faced by investigative journalists and the complex interplay between personal and professional lives in high-pressure professions.

As we remember Le Hoang Hung — a dedicated journalist, loving father, and seeker of truth — we are reminded of the importance of press freedom and the need for societies to protect those who dare to speak truth to power. Though the courts have rendered their verdict, the full truth behind this dark mystery may never be fully known.
